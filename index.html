Community Garden Eviction Threatens to Shrink Green Space for Residents

Smiling Hogshead Ranch must vacate by November's end, reducing green space in the industrial district.

By Niamh McAuliffe

November 23, 2024

Smiling Hogshead Ranch is unlike other community gardens, with no fence and no waiting list, people can join and get their hands dirty anytime they like… until November 30 according to an eviction notice issued by the MTA earlier this month. Currently the eviction may be on hold according to the gardens community board, but if executed the second community district in Queens will have access to even less green space. According to data collected by the 2010 census, only 59 percent of residents live within walking distance of a park or open space.

“The fact that there's no other green space in the area, in this industrial area,” said Annabelle Meunier, who has been a member of the garden for nine years. “It gives us a place to sit down, a place to enjoy nature.”
